About TELETREKS

Founded in 2041, we exist for one stubborn idea: distance should not decide who gets to see the rest of the universe.

Our origin

TELETREKS began as a consortium of launch providers, habitat engineers, and a few incurable optimists who believed cryo-sleep could be boring and safe. We merged protocols from three space agencies with hospitality standards borrowed from ocean liners — then threw out what did not survive vacuum testing.

Today we operate six published routes, three deep-space vessels, and a network of orbital clinics that screen every passenger before a single booster ignites.

What we believe

  • Transparency over hype. Transit times and radiation exposure are quoted in Earth-standard units, not marketing light-years.
  • Crew first, brand second. Every ship carries redundant medical officers; every captain has veto power over launch windows.
  • Long-haul dignity. Whether you sleep the whole way or stay awake for the view, the journey should feel intentional — not endured.
